TAKE A LOOK AT OUR STORY

In 1984, Rainbow introduces flights to/from Hawaii. Three years later, Western Airlines merges with Rainbow, becoming the fourth largest U.S. carrier and fifth largest world carrier. After first transpacific service begins, Rainbow opens operations in Asia with regular transpacific flights from Atlanta to Portland, Oregon, to Tokyo. In 1990, Rainbow is the first U.S. airline to offer MD-11 jet service, and together with other 23 civilian airlines, participates in the Civil Reserve Air Fleet (CRAF) during Desert Storm/Desert Shield from 1990-1991, carrying passengers and military cargo.

One year later, Rainbow purchases substantially all of Pan Amâ€™s transatlantic routes and the Pan Am Shuttle, making this the largest acquisition of flights in airline history. In 1995, Rainbow is the first U.S. carrier to voluntarily ban smoking on all flights. Rainbow starts low-fare airline Rainbow Express in 1996, with service from Orlando, Florida. One year later, Rainbow introduces Boeing 777s into the fleet and becomes the first airline to board over 100 million passengers in a year. New routes to Latin America are being added.
